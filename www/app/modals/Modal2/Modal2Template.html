1. I've wasted so much time, I should have started this earlier.

What happened in the past is in the past, and shouldn't poison my present. I still have control of what I do now and give it my best.

2. There's not enough time to complete this task and do it well so there's no point starting.

I can still try hard and starting now means I'll definitely do better than if I don't start.

3. I don't know where to start, there's so much to be done.

There's no "right" place to start, the best thing I can do is just start somewhere.

4. This task is very important and I can't afford to do badly at it which stresses me out.

I actually can't guarantee the outcome of this task anyway. I can only give my best at this point in time and hope that it turns out well.

5. I don't feel like starting this task right now, I'll feel more like doing it later today/tomorrow.

If I'm honest with myself I know tomorrow will likely be no different than today. If I want to get this task done, the only option is to start it right now.

6. There's enough time left I can start it later.

There have been many times when I've underestimated how long tasks take, it's better to start it now and not take a risk.

7. The task scares me, I don't know if I have the skills to do it well.

It's definitely possible that my skills are not where I want them to be, and that's ok, all I can do now is give it my best.

8. I think I won't do well because I've failed at this task before.

Just because something happened in the past does not mean that it will happen the same way again.

9. I'm just not good at this task, there's no point trying.

I'm not good at this task right now, but that just means I have the opportunity to grow and become better.
